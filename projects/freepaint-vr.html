Freepaint VR

Tools: Unity, C#, Blender Role: Solo Developer

Overview

Freepaint VR is an interactive virtual reality experience developed for the Meta Quest, designed to provide a creative and therapeutic outlet for children, parents, and carers at the Ronald McDonald House in Kwun Tong, Hong Kong. The project transforms a familiar space into a vibrant canvas, allowing users to paint, mix colours, and express themselves freely. Developed with input from the charity, Freepaint VR prioritises accessibility, intuitive controls, and a joyful, immersive environment.

The Challenge

Children and families staying at the Ronald McDonald House often face immense emotional and physical challenges due to prolonged hospital visits and treatments. Many children, especially those undergoing cancer treatment, struggle with limited mobility or fatigue. The goal was to create a VR experience that:

  • Provides a mess-free, creative space for self-expression.
  • Offers intuitive and accessible controls for children with varying physical abilities.
  • Encourages exploration and discovery through engaging features.

The Solution

Freepaint VR combines paint mixing and 3D drawing mechanics in a virtual art gallery filled with familiar characters from Disney and other children's animations. Key features include:

Paint Mixing: Players can mix colours to create new ones, fostering a sense of discovery and experimentation.

3D Drawing: Players can draw freely in the space around them, encouraging creativity without constraints.

Immersive Environment: The virtual art gallery features beloved characters, creating a comforting and engaging space for children of all ages.

Accessible Controls: The typical motion controller can be paired with directional joystick inputs, ensuring the experience is comfortable for users with limited mobility.

Technical Implementation

Platform: Developed in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints

Painting Mechanics: Spline meshes were used for their intuitiveness. Drawn strokes appear exactly where the player's hand is, eliminating the complexity of calculating angles from spray cans onto surfaces.

Colour Mixing: Implemented using vector interpolation to blend colours smoothly and realistically.

Optimisation: Prioritised smooth, responsive controls to ensure a frustration-free experience on the Meta Quest.

Impact

Freepaint VR was presented to the Ronald McDonald House Charity, where it received positive feedback:

  • Engagement: The paint mixing mechanic was praised for its appeal to both younger and older children, offering complexity for those who wanted it while remaining accessible to all.
  • Accessibility: The adjustable controls and minimal on-screen text made the experience inclusive for children with varying abilities.
  • Therapeutic Value: The immersive environment and familiar characters provided a comforting and joyful escape for families during a challenging time.
